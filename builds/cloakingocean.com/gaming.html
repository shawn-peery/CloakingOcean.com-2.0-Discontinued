Gaming | My Favorite Past Time

Gaming Introduction

Since the beginning of my childhood, I've held a controller in my hands! My older brother and sister were avid gamers before me and I followed in their footsteps. I mainly played on Nintendo systems and eventually Xbox 360 games when I was younger. These days, I do most of my gaming on the computer with occasional breaks on other systems.

Early Nintendo

My brother has a wide arrangement of classic nintendo consoles such as the NES (Nintendo Entertainment System), SNES (Super Nintendo Entertainment System), Nintendo 64, and Gamecube! My favorite game for the NES has to be Contra! It's a side scroller shooter that also has a small trick you can use to gain 30 lives compared to the 3 lives you start out with! You face an army of opponents while dying if shot only once!

I'd say my favorite game from the SNES system is Super Mario World! The adventurous theme of this game makes any new player excited to explore! A major part of the game I personally enjoy is the implementation of Yoshi's. Mario can ride on their back and different colored Yoshi's have different unique abilities! For example, the red Yoshi spits fire when it spits something out while the blue Yoshi flies into the air when it has something in it's mouth!

I'm unsure if I have a specific favorite when it comes to Nintendo 64 games. However, my top favorites are Banjo Kazooie, Super Mario 64, and Gauntlet Legends! Banjo Kazooie has an amazing aethetic design along with various different abilities and guns the duo can use! The story line is very compelling as well! Super Mario 64 was a very popular game when it came out due to it's impressive implementation of 3D platforming! Finally, Gauntlet Legends is a game about restoring balance after a massive outbreak of a very dangerous character! You must explore all of the different realms and restore power in each to be able to fight the final boss! It's a 4-player game with a ton of fun mechanics!

Once again, it's difficult for me to choose a specific favorite for the Gamecube because it has so many brilliant games! The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker, Luigi's Mansion, and Pikmin are my top 3 favorites! The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker has an amazing cartoonish art style! It's a huge shift from the previous Legend of Zelda games! One major game mechanic of this game is sailing in a boat to get across vast ocean and reach islands sprinkled throughout the entire map! Although that may sound tedious, this game gives you the ability to change direction of the wind! Thus, you can sail anywhere you want with ease! This game also has a ton of puzzles and story! I'd highly recommend playing this game if possible! Luigi's Mansion is a great twist of the classic "Mario saves the day" theme. In this story, Luigi is the one saving Mario! With the help of a genius professor, you are equiped with ghost capturing machines to rid a newly created mansion of all its ghosts! There are also sequels of this game as well that I would recommend as well! And finally, Pikmin is a game all about returning home. When the main character, Olimar, crash lands on a distant plant and all hope seems lost, he discovers small creatues he names Pikmin who eventually help him retrieve his ship parts and return home! It's exciting to build up an army and defeat your foes easily when making use of each pikmin's abilities!

Xbox 360

With the generally more mature games available on the Xbox 360, it was a hard sell for my father. However, he was eventually convinced and my first games consisted of Viva Piñata and Banjo Kazooie Nuts & Bolts! Viva Piñata personally was a let down because its gameplay was nothing like the TV show! It felt more like someone made a game and made it look like Viva Piñata. For Banjo Kazooie Nuts & Bolts, however, I found I enjoyed it much more than the majority. Even though it was a major change in the franchise, it introduced gameplay that was still exciting and entertaining! The whole point of the game is to create vehicles and obtain upgrades to make the best machine for whatever challenge you're facing! It's a very creative game and I enjoyed making my own design of carts, boats, and planes!

Eventually, a friend introduced me to the game Minecraft! Instantly, I was hooked! Minecraft is a game about survival and creativity. Most of the world consists of square blocks which can be harvested and collected. It's a very open-ended game because there's no real "end" to the game. Even if you beat the final boss named the "Ender Dragon", you just continue onwards! The player vs. player component was also very exciting! On some days, my friends and I would split up and see who could muster up the most resources and finish off the others! Back in this time, there was no creative mode which allowed people to have infinite building blocks! However, there was a duplication glitch that allowed players to do some crazy things! My friend later showed me the PC version of Minecraft and I was amazed at all the different creatures and creative mode options! This is what eventually led me to PC gaming!

PC Gaming

My first gaming computer, a Mac Book Pro, was sold to me by a friend at a very generous price! It was the first time I'd used a Mac computer before! It took quite a long time to get use to. However, the first thing I did on that computer was install Minecraft! My friend and I actually bought each other Minecraft! I was so excited to play around with the in-game commands, additional mobs, and creative mode! Shortly after, my friend and I joined public servers and learned more about the plugins in use in most servers! Like I mentioned on the Java page, this is what led my programming desire to learn more about plugins and create my own! Minecraft has always been one of my favorite games. Although, recently it's become more of a creative space to create plugins than an actual game for me.

League of Legends was another major game my friends and I played! League is a MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena) which consists of 2 teams with 5 people each! Each team has a nexus which if destroyed wins the game for the other team! There's many different mechanics to learn and master. First of all are the wide character or champion pool. Additionally, you'd gain money to buy items that would specifically boost your champion. For me, the learning curve seemed quite steep and I didn't play very seriously. But eventually, after enough time playing and pratice, I really enjoy playing it and improving my skills as well!

Steam was always a program I heard about but I knew nothing of at this point. A relative showed me the ropes and all the various games he enjoyed playing. My personal favorites over the years have been Garry's Mod, Super Win the Game, and Titan Souls! Garry's Mod, similarly to Minecraft, had a very creative aspect to it! There were many custom created characters and items! And there were offcial game modes servers could host. My favorites were Trouble in Terrorist Town and Prop Hunt! Super Win the Game has an amazing retro graphic and mechanic style! It was very nostalgic for me and I'm glad that with modern technology, annoying bugs are much less frequent. It was a nice jump back into time when things felt much more simplier as a kid. And finally Titan Souls is an amazing game! Your character is very small compared the the enormous beasts and monsters you face! This game requires a bit of skill to dodge and land your shots accurately. But the payoff feels amazing! Taking down something 10 times your size with a single arrow shot!
